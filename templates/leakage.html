Leakage
When IV infiltration occurs, fluid from the IV can begin to leak out of the puncture site and onto the surface of the skin. A cellulose detection pad absorbs this leaked medication and changes color: an indication to the patient and staff that the IV has failed. Dye color change is visible at ~0.1mL infiltrated fluid, therefore leakage events are caught early before they become serious.
A healthy IV site will show no color change. But the moment leakage occurs, the IV site will turn bright blue: a clear sign that it needs replacing.
